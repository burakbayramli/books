Ball Rolling Down a Hill:
BallHill.h , BallHill.cpp , PhysicsModule.h , PhysicsModule.cpp , BallHill.mpg

A ball is placed at the top of a hill whose shape is an elliptical paraboloid. The hill is assumed to be frictionless. The only force acting on the ball is gravitational force. The ball is slightly pushed so that it may slide down the hill. The second image shows the path of the center of the ball as it rolls down the hill. The ball rotates at a speed commensurate with its downhill velocity.
