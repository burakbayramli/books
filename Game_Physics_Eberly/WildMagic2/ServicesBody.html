Magic Software provides various services to the technical community. Our main areas of expertise are computer graphics (including games programming), image analysis, and numerical methods. We provide contract programming, on-site consulting, and training in these areas. We also provide support services such as performance analysis, testing, and debugging of applications.

A current and ongoing project is algorithm development for the Medical Image Processing, Analysis, and Visualization package that is developed by researchers at the National Institutes of Health. The package is written in Java and Java3D. Recent algorithms that we provided for the package:
  • Level surface extraction. Uses either a Marching Tetrahedra algorithm or a topologically correct algorithm for rectangular lattices.

  • Decimation of triangle meshes. The meshes have millions of triangles. Uses a Garland-Heckbert-style algorithm.

  • Level surface viewer. A Java3D application for viewing lit triangle meshes that represent level surfaces.

  • Volume rendering. Based on software ray tracing.

  • Volume rendering. Based on shear-warp factorization.

  • Brain extraction tool. A coarse-level 3D magnetic resonance image segmentation called skull stripping.

  • Adaptive skeleton climbing. Postprocessing an extracted level surface mesh using triangle decimation is time consuming since the meshes tend to have on the order of one million triangles. Skeleton climbing allows you to quickly build a multiresolution level surface during the extraction phase. The multiple resolutions are constructed by a bottom-up voxel merging method that is based on sign changes of the voxel data minus the desired level surface value.

  • Volume rendering using hardware-supported 3D texturing.

  • Virtual colonoscopy. A 3D graphics application that allows a user to do a fly-through of the tube surface obtained from a segmentation of a colonoscopy data set. The tube is vertex colored, the colors associated with the mean curvature of the surface. The goal is to identify polyps that are potentially malignant. Such polyps show up as large curvature protrusions/invaginations of the surface. The vertex coloring helps draw the user's attention when performing the fly-through.

  • Fine-level segmentation and analysis of the cortical surface. The input is a segmented, skull-stripped brain data set. The segmentation used a fuzzy C-means algorithm to identify white matter, gray matter, cerebral spinal fluid, and background. A surface evolution scheme is used to produce a good quality triangle mesh that approximates the folds of the brain. The evolution is set up to prevent self-intersection of the mesh. The final surface is then mapped to a sphere (called surface inflation to allow a user to study regions of interest of the cortical surface. The mapping is conformal (local angles are preserved) and designed to minimize metric distortion. This allows a spherical coordinate system to be assigned to the cortical surface.
Algorithms for this project currently in development include:
  • Viewport-aligned volume rendering. Use hardware-supported texturing, but software-generated image slices that are aligned to the view direction.

  • 3D Face anonymizer. Modify enough information of a 3D image of the head to prevent a user of a surface or volume renderer from recognizing the face.

  • Virtual endoscopy A fly-through of tube surface with branches, for example, brachial tubes. This project also involves occlusion culling of the segmented data set to speed up the rendering during the fly-through.

  • Nonlinear registration of 3D images. Two 3D images, an affine registration is performed; rotation, translation, and scaling are used to register the images. This registration is used as the starting point for another registration that is based on B-spline volume functions.
Selected projects that we have also worked on include:
  • Least-squares fitting of data by ellipsoids to assist in the calibration of devices that make 3D measurements dependent on the Earth's magnetic field.

  • Modeling of coalition behavior of political parties. The parties are represented as 3D ellipsoids in a feature space, in the style of statistical pattern recognizition where the features are party beliefs. Coalitions are possible if the ellipsoids intersect. We produced algorithms and code in Java for fast and accurate intersection of convex polyhedra that represent ellipsoids and intersection of ellipsoids.

  • Debugging and performance testing/improvment for a 3D F1 car racing game for a prominent game company.

  • Development and implementation of 2D facial animations in the style of nonphotorealistic rendering.
